Inflation

Pros of this chart are that it shows a 5 color gradient change defined by my specific hex codes. I can see the data points for each day the inflation rate is calculated by month. Based on the data, there is a big inflation drop in early 2020 which aligns with the start of the pandemic. When the confidence in the economy and the presidential election proceeded, the economy began making more investments and purchases and market is flooded with liquidity causing inflation to raise. The Federal reserve enacted a series of rate hikes to cool the market and decrease spending.

Using Tableau, this chart can use a similar gradient color action to give a red value if high inflation and green when it’s low. The con of this chart is that I cannot add more color options that are defined by the average or median, max and min values, as I can with d3.js. The pro for tableau is that I can combine charts and have a slider interact with multiple static charts

This chart was completed in Tableau where I can utilize a similar design and change the data via drag and drop. While it’s east to implement, we have less control on the colors.

In Tableau, this chart shows the green colors and red colors in correlation with each other over time. When the FED Rates begin to increase, we can see the Tech Layoff counts increase immensely from September 2022 and onwards
